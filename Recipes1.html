Ratatouille

Ingredients

  • 2 medium eggplants, diced into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 zucchinis, sliced into half-moons
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 ripe tomatoes, peeled and diced
  • a couple of garlic cloves, minced
  • a splash of olive oil
  • a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • a handful of fresh basil leaves, torn

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) to get it ready for baking.
  2. Heat a splash of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat, then sauté the onions and garlic until they’re soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the diced eggplants, zucchinis, and bell peppers to the skillet, stirring occasionally, until they start to soften, roughly 10 minutes.
  4. Mix in the diced tomatoes, a pinch of salt, and black pepper, then let everything simmer together for another 5 minutes to blend the flavors.
  5. Transfer the vegetable mixture to a baking dish, cover with foil, and bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes.
  6. Remove the foil, sprinkle the torn basil leaves over the top, and bake for an additional 10 minutes to let the flavors meld and the top slightly brown.
  7. Tip: Let the Ratatouille sit for 5 minutes after baking to allow the juices to redistribute. Tip: For an extra layer of flavor, drizzle with a bit more olive oil before serving. Tip: Serve with a crusty baguette to soak up the delicious juices.

Tiana’s Beignets from The Princess and the Frog

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • a couple of tablespoons of granulated sugar
  • a pinch of salt
  • 1 packet of active dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup of warm milk (about 110°F)
  • a splash of vanilla extract
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons of melted butter
  • vegetable oil for frying
  • a generous dusting of powdered sugar

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast.
  2. Add the warm milk, vanilla extract, egg, and melted butter to the dry ingredients. Mix until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5 minutes, until smooth and elastic. Tip: If the dough sticks to your hands, add a little more flour, but don’t overdo it to keep the beignets light.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  5. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/4-inch thickness. Cut into 2-inch squares.
  6. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 370°F. Tip: Use a candy thermometer to ensure the oil is at the perfect temperature for golden beignets.
  7. Fry the beignets in batches, turning once, until puffed and golden brown, about 2 minutes per side. Tip: Don’t overcrowd the pot to maintain the oil temperature.
  8. Drain on paper towels and dust liberally with powdered sugar while still warm.

Merida’s Oatcakes from Brave

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of rolled oats
  • A splash of warm water
  • A couple of tablespoons of honey
  • A pinch of salt
  • A drizzle of olive oil

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the rolled oats and a pinch of salt until well combined.
  3. Add a splash of warm water and a couple of tablespoons of honey to the oat mixture, stirring until the ingredients start to come together into a dough. Tip: The dough should be sticky but manageable; if it’s too dry, add a bit more water.
  4. Lightly flour your hands and shape the dough into small, flat rounds about 1/4 inch thick. Tip: For uniform oatcakes, use a cookie cutter or the rim of a glass to shape them.
  5. Place the oatcakes on the prepared baking sheet and drizzle lightly with olive oil.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and crisp. Tip: Keep an eye on them after 15 minutes to prevent over-browning.
