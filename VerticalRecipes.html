Ariel Pasta

Ingredients

  • 1 box of pasta shells.
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper.
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots.
  • 1/2 cup halved cherry tomatoes.
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese.
  • 2 Tbsp snipped chives.
  • 1 cup Italian salad dressing.
  • 1/4 tsp salt.
  • 1/4 tsp ground pepper.

Directions

  1. Prepare the pasta according to the directions on the box.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked pasta shells, red pepper, carrots, cherry tomatoes, Parmesan, and chives. Stir with a wooden spoon to mix them.
  3. Pour the Italian salad dressing over the pasta, and sprinkle on the salt and pepper. Stir again until all the ingredients are evenly coated.
  4. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and chill until serving time.

Beef Ragout from Beauty and the Beast

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs of beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • A couple of carrots, diced
  • A couple of celery stalks, diced
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • A splash of red wine
  • 2 cups of beef broth
  • A handful of fresh thyme
  • 2 tbsp of tomato paste
  • A drizzle of olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to your liking

Directions

  1. Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat until shimmering.
  2. Season the beef cubes generously with salt and pepper, then brown them in batches to avoid overcrowding, about 5 minutes per batch. Tip: Don’t rush the browning; it’s key for depth of flavor.
  3. Remove the beef and set aside. In the same pot, add the diced carrots, celery, and onion, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for another minute to deepen the flavor.
  5. Pour in a splash of red wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot—this is where the magic happens!
  6. Return the beef to the pot, add the beef broth and thyme, then bring to a simmer. Tip: For the best results, let it simmer gently, not boil.
  7. Cover and cook on low heat for about 2 hours, or until the beef is fork-tender. Tip: Check occasionally to ensure the liquid hasn’t reduced too much; add a bit more broth if needed.
  8. Once done, taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if necessary.

Snow White’s Gooseberry Pie

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of fresh gooseberries, topped and tailed
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar, plus a sprinkle more for dusting
  • A splash of lemon juice
  • A couple of tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt
  • 2 pie crusts (homemade or store-bought), because we’re all about that flaky life
  • 1 egg, beaten, for that golden shine

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) to get it nice and toasty for the pie.
  2. In a large bowl, gently toss the gooseberries with sugar, lemon juice, cornstarch, vanilla extract, and salt until the berries are evenly coated. Tip: Let the mixture sit for 10 minutes to draw out the juices.
  3. Roll out one pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish, trimming any excess dough but leaving a bit of overhang for sealing.
  4. Pour the gooseberry filling into the crust, spreading it out evenly. Tip: Dot the top with small pieces of butter for extra richness.
  5. Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the filling. Trim, seal, and crimp the edges. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  6. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg and sprinkle lightly with sugar for a sparkling finish.
  7. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. Tip: Place a baking sheet underneath to catch any drips.

Snow White’s Gooseberry Pie

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of hazelnuts, toasted and skins removed
  • 4 cups of vegetable broth, for a rich base
  • A splash of heavy cream, to add silkiness
  • A couple of garlic cloves, minced for a hint of sharpness
  • 1 tbsp of olive oil, for sautéing
  • Salt, just enough to enhance the flavors

Directions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat, then sauté the minced garlic until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  2. Add the toasted hazelnuts to the pot, stirring to coat them in the garlic-infused oil.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth, bringing the mixture to a gentle boil before reducing the heat to simmer for 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  4. Carefully transfer the soup to a blender, blending until smooth. Tip: For an extra smooth texture, strain the soup through a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Return the soup to the pot, stirring in the heavy cream and salt to taste. Heat through for another 5 minutes on low heat.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with a drizzle of cream or a sprinkle of crushed hazelnuts for added texture.
